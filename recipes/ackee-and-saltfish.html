Ackee and Saltfish Recipe

Description

Ackee and Saltfish is the national dish of Jamaica, known for its unique combination of flavors and vibrant appearance. Ackee, a fruit with a buttery texture, is saut√©ed with salted codfish, onions, tomatoes, and bell peppers, creating a savory and slightly tangy dish. Often seasoned with scotch bonnet peppers and fresh herbs, this dish is typically enjoyed with boiled green bananas, dumplings, or fried plantains. Ackee and Saltfish is a beloved breakfast or brunch staple, offering a true taste of Jamaican culinary heritage.

Ingredients

  • Salted Cod
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Yellow Onion
  • Bell Pepper
  • Scotch Bonnet Pepper
  • Garlic
  • Dried Thyme Leaves
  • Smoked Paprika
  • Tomato
  • Scallions
  • Ackee
  • Kosher Salt & Black Pepper

Steps

  1. Place cod in a bowl and cover with cold water. Let it soak, changing the water every few minutes.
  2. Discard soaking water and place the cod in a saucepan.
  3. Cover with fresh water and a lid, and bring to a boil.
  4. Discard boiling water, cover with fresh water, and bring to a boil once more.
  5. Once cooked, remove the fish from the saucepan. It should flake easily.
  6. Break the fish up into small pieces with two forks.
  7. Add onion, scallion whites and light greens, bell pepper and Scotch bonnet pepper to a nonstick skillet with oil. Cook until softened and translucent.
  8. Add garlic, thyme, and paprika and cook again.
  9. Add cod and cook for a few minutes.
  10. Add tomatoes and cook until all vegetables are tender.
  11. Add ackee and cook until heated through.
  12. Turn off the heat, stir in dark greens of scallions, and season with salt and pepper.
